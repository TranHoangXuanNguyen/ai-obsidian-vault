## What is an AI assistant?

An AI assistant, also known as an artificial intelligence assistant, refers to any digital tool or application that **utilizes generative artificial intelligence technologies** to perform specific tasks, answer questions, or carry out commands.

AI assistants are frequently cloud-based, so you can access them virtually anywhere, as long as you have a stable internet connection.

## How does an AI assistant work?

AI assistants use artificial intelligence, more specifically, generative AI technology. Generative AI creates new content or data based on patterns and examples by using machine learning.

[Generative AI models](https://thebusinessdive.com/openai-competitors) use text input for text generation, image generation, video creation, or music composition. Due to **machine learning**, AI assistants can learn from past interactions, improving the accuracy of their responses over time.

Generally, there are two ways how you can interact with [generative AI](https://thebusinessdive.com/generative-ai-companies) tools. It can follow voice commands by utilizing natural language processing for speech recognition. Also, AI assistants are capable of understanding text messages.

For instance, ChatGPT understands text messages, while a Google assistant uses voice commands.

Cloud computing is another crucial technology that plays a significant role. AI assistants can access extensive data to handle various tasks and deliver instant feedback to your queries.

## What are the categories of AI assistants, and how can you use them

There are more types of AI assistants depending on the type of tasks and their functionalities:

### AI personal assistant (AI virtual assistant)

If you hear the terms like AI virtual assistant, automated personal assistant, or intelligent personal assistant, just remember they all refer to the same thing: AI personal assistants.

An **AI personal assistant is software designed to understand natural language voice and text commands** and carry out tasks for the users. It focuses on tasks mostly handled by human personal assistants or secretaries in the past.

One of the most common types is the AI voice assistant. It has the ability of speech recognition, and you can communicate with it by voice interaction in multiple languages. I am sure you have come across names like Google Assistant, Alexa, and Siri before, as they are some of the most popular AI voice assistants out there.

People who use “AI assistant” and “personal AI assistant” or “AI virtual assistants” are often used interchangeably. However, they are not the same.

An **AI assistant has broader functionalities, while personal AI assistants are often designed to support an individual’s daily activities, work, or lifestyle.** People use AI personal assistants for various tasks. Personal AI assistants are great for increasing productivity, controlling your smart devices, and capable of sending messages, and making phone calls.

## AI assistant vs. chatbot

You might wonder whether an **AI assistant and chatbot are the same or what is the difference between them**.

Good question. Let’s break it down.

### Rule-based chatbot and AI-powered chatbot

There are two types of chatbots. Traditional chatbots are rule-based chatbots, while there are AI-powered chatbots. AI-powered chatbots use conversational AI technology, which is quite similar to the technology used by AI assistants.

Conversational AI includes a wide range of AI solutions that utilize data, machine learning, and natural language processing to provide voice and text-based output. This technology helps AI powered-chatbots or AI assistants naturally interact with humans.

For example, ChatGPT is a conversational AI chatbot, while many of the chatbot used for customer service is mainly rule-based chatbot.

From a technology perspective, there is no significant difference between AI assistants and AI-powered chatbots.

Nevertheless, there are **major differences between AI assistants and traditional rule-based chatbots**.

### What difference between an AI assistant and a rule-based chatbot

While chatbots and AI assistants use natural language processing to understand the intent behind the user’s queries, there are essential differences.

Chatbot has a rule-based structure with predefined answers that can mostly handle frequently asked questions. AI assistants have the ability to answer more complex questions and make recommendations using machine learning.

Furthermore, the chatbot mainly provides robotic answers, while an AI assistant can answer in a more human-like way.

In general, chatbots are great when it comes to handling simple problems. However, **AI assistants can deal more effectively with complex issues.**
